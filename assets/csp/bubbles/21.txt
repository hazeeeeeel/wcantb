The famous phrase “The medium is the message” was coined by Marshall McLuhan in his groundbreaking book, Understanding Media: The Extensions of Man (1964). In this work, McLuhan explores the effects of media on human perception and society, arguing that the medium—the technology or form through which content is delivered—has a far greater impact on society than the content it conveys. What people wear and use tells what they value when they make decisions: practicality, brands, quality, personal values, etc.
